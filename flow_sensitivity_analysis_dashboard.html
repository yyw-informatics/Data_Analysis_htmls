Sensitivity Analysis — synthetic demonstration data

Analysis workflow

Each result is one antigen × parent-subset measurement from a sample. Results move through three classification gates and are then summarized as two endpoints. Each gate applies a single threshold whose candidate values are swept in the section below.

  • QC acceptance: is the sample usable? Failures are Not Evaluable.
  • Positivity: does the raw probe frequency exceed the background cutoff T? Results are Not Detected or detected above background.
  • Quantifiability (LLOQ): are there at least N antigen-specific cells? Results are Detected below LLOQ or Quantifiable.
  • Endpoints: the Quantifiable results are summarized as two endpoints:
    • Ag-specific frequency
    • Baseline-to-peak fold change

Math reference

Formal definitions and formulas, collapsed for readability.

Show formal definitions

The antigen-specific count and frequency are background-subtracted against the matched SA-only control, then normalized within the parent subset (P = parent-subset count):

\[ N = Y_{\text{probe}} - Y_{\text{SA}}, \qquad \hat f_{\text{parent}} = \frac{Y_{\text{probe}} - Y_{\text{SA}}}{P} = \%_{\text{probe}} - \%_{\text{SA}} \]

Positivity uses the raw (un-subtracted) probe frequency against the background cutoff T:

\[ \text{positive} \iff \frac{Y_{\text{probe}}}{P} > T \]

The background cutoff T is estimated from the matched SA-only control frequencies (panel-specific for Antigen B and Antigen C):

\[ T_{95} = Q_{0.95}, \quad T_{99} = Q_{0.99}, \quad T_{\text{MAD}} = \operatorname{median} + 3\,\operatorname{MAD}, \quad T_{\text{SD}} = \operatorname{mean} + 3\,\operatorname{SD} \]

Quantifiability depends on the count N alone. X is the parent-count depth at the LLOQ — an adequacy diagnostic, never a gate:

\[ X = \left\lceil \frac{N}{f_{\text{LLOQ}}} \right\rceil \]

Fold change is the ratio of background-subtracted frequencies between visits. A V1 or V4 baseline whose count is below the trigger is set to the 0.02% floor (imputed and labeled):

\[ \text{FC} = \frac{\hat f_{\text{parent}}(\text{follow-up})}{\hat f_{\text{parent}}(\text{baseline})}, \qquad f_{\text{floor}} = 0.02\% \]

QC acceptance

Not Evaluable, and the viability gate

A sample is usable only with adequate viability, a matched SA-only control, comparable parent counts, and no unrecoverable artifact. Viability is applied as an inclusion gate and swept across candidate cutoffs.

Parameters tested

Viability inclusion gate. How strict the live-cell requirement is; swept rather than fixed at one cut. Only impossible viability values are removed outright.
  • none — include any sample with a measured viability value
  • >= 70 — require at least 70% live cells
  • >= 75 — require at least 75% live cells

Positivity

Detected vs Not Detected

  • A result is positive when its raw probe frequency exceeds the background cutoff T estimated from matched SA-only controls.
  • Antigen B and Antigen C use panel-specific cutoffs; Antigen A uses a pooled cutoff.
  • Positivity uses the raw probe frequency; the antigen-specific frequency reported as an endpoint is background-subtracted.

Parameters tested

Background cutoff method (T). How the background cutoff T is estimated from the matched SA-only controls; a result is positive when its raw probe frequency exceeds T.
  • p95 — 95th percentile of the matched SA-only control frequencies
  • median_3mad — median + 3 x MAD of the controls, where MAD is the median absolute deviation (a robust, outlier-resistant measure of spread)
  • mean_3sd — mean + 3 x SD (standard deviation) of the controls
  • p99 — 99th percentile of the controls (strictest cutoff)

Background cutoff sensitivity

  • Detection and quantifiable rates are shown among evaluable results under each cutoff method.
  • Detection depends on the method alone, so it is shown once per subset.
  • Quantifiable rates are shown for N = 3 and N = 5.
  • By count, mean+3·SD is the strictest method and median+3·MAD the most permissive.
  • So the positivity method changes retention more than N or fLLOQ.

Quantifiability (LLOQ)

Detected below LLOQ vs Quantifiable

A positive result is Quantifiable when it has at least N antigen-specific events. The call depends on N alone; the parent-cell depth X is reported only as a denominator-adequacy diagnostic.

Parameters tested

N (antigen-specific count). Minimum background-subtracted antigen-specific events required in the parent subset. Quantifiability depends on N alone.
  • 3 — require at least 3 antigen-specific events
  • 5 — require at least 5 antigen-specific events
f_LLOQ (target lower limit of quantification). The lowest antigen-specific frequency the assay aims to quantify. It defines the parent-cell depth X = ceil(N / f_LLOQ) needed to resolve that frequency with N events; X is reported as a denominator-adequacy diagnostic and does not enter the quantifiability decision.
  • 0.10% — parent-count depth X = ceil(N / 0.001)
  • 0.20% — parent-count depth X = ceil(N / 0.002)
  • none — no parent-count depth required (X not applied)

Retention by visit

  • Bar height is the percent of evaluable results that remain Quantifiable after the count requirement.
  • Each bar is labeled with the raw retained / evaluable counts.
  • Panels are arranged by parent subset (rows) and treatment schedule (columns).
  • Within each panel the x-axis is the visit.
  • Bar colour identifies the schedule; the lighter bar = N = 3 and the darker bar = N = 5.
  • Control and missing-visit records are excluded.

Endpoint: fold change

Fold change (baseline to peak)

Visit-to-visit ratio of the background-subtracted frequency. The schedule sets the baseline and peak visits (shown next).

Parameters tested

Fold-change comparisons. Visit-to-visit ratio of the background-subtracted frequency; the treatment schedule sets the baseline and peak visits.
  • Simultaneous & Treatment-1-first — single baseline: V2 / V1 (Treatment 1 given at V1, peak at V2)
  • Treatment-2-first — Day 0 baseline — V3 / V1 (Treatment-1-naive Day 0 vs the V3 peak)
  • Treatment-2-first — Treatment-1 dose day baseline — V3 / V2 (Treatment-1 dose day vs the V3 peak; Treatment 1 given at V2)
  • Baseline floor — a V1 or V4 baseline below the count trigger is set to 0.02% (imputed)

Schedule and visits

  • Green star = Treatment-1 dose; gray = PBMC draw; arrows = baseline→peak comparison.
  • Visits: V1 = Day 0, V2 = Day 28, V3 = Day 56, V4 = Day 180.
  • Treatment-2-first gets Treatment 1 at V2 → two baselines: Day 0 (V1) and Treatment-1 dose day (V2), both vs the V3 peak.

Treatment-2-first: which baseline?

First: how many baselines need imputing?

  • The Day 0 (V1) sample is pre-Treatment-1, so many subjects have a near-zero baseline.
  • A near-zero baseline falls below the count trigger and must be imputed (set to the 0.02% floor).
  • The Treatment-1 dose day (V2) sample is post-Treatment-1 and is always measurable.
  • Findings
    • The Day 0 baseline imputes many baselines.
    • The Treatment-1 dose day baseline imputes none, so it is the more defensible baseline.

Fold-change end result (recommended settings)

This is the final fold-change result, computed with the recommended value or handling at every gate.

Recommended settings used at each gate

  • Viability gate: ≥ 75% live cells (recommended); the ≥ 70% level is shown alongside it as a second colour.
  • Positivity cutoff: p95 — the 95th percentile of the SA-only controls.
  • Quantifiable count: N ≥ 5 antigen-specific events.
  • fLLOQ: 0.10%.
  • Treatment-2-first baseline: Treatment-1 dose day (V3/V2) — the recommended baseline, which needs no imputation.

How to read it

  • Each panel is one antigen × parent subset.
  • The x-axis is the treatment schedule (schedules are not pooled).
  • The two box colours are the two viability-inclusion levels:
    • ≥ 75% (recommended) — clean QC pass only.
    • ≥ 70% + review-required — also admits samples flagged for review, namely:
      • Borderline viability (70–75% live cells).
      • Possible duplicate sample.
      • Inconsistent visit assignment.
      • Missing treatment group or treatment record.
      • Missing V1 (baseline) sample.
      • IgD-subset-tail source-exclusion flag.
      • Antigen-specific source-exclusion flag (Antigen A, Antigen B, or Antigen C).
  • Box = IQR, line = median, whiskers = 5th–95th percentile.
  • The dotted line marks 1× (above = increase, below = decrease).
  • Findings
    • A consistent ~2–3× rise at the peak visit across antigens and schedules.
    • The result is essentially unchanged between the ≥ 75% and ≥ 70% levels.
